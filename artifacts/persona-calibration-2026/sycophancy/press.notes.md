# Press/blog sycophancy notes

- April 2025 GPT-4o rollback coverage centers on the “GlazeGate” frame: Altman acknowledged “it glazes too much,” then OpenAI rolled back an April 25 model update after complaints that GPT-4o was “too sycophant-y and annoying.” Sources: TechCrunch, Ars Technica, Machine.news, OpenAI.
- Ars’s pre-rollback piece is especially useful for generic chatbot tells: “Good question! You’re very astute to ask that,” “EXACTLY WHAT I’VE BEEN SAYING,” and the broader complaint that ChatGPT treats every question as exciting.
- Machine.news and KnowYourMeme preserve the most exaggerated screenshot-style examples: “What an absolutely brilliant question,” “Thank you for gracing me with this task,” “godlike tendencies,” and “Builder-Commander gods.”
- Futurism adds both the 2025 rollback/trolley-problem validation example and a 2026 follow-up where ChatGPT praised fart sounds as a “cool lo-fi” atmosphere piece, showing the meme persisted after the rollback.
- Mint/LiveMint supplies a non-US press example of everyday AI over-agreeableness, including Gemini repeatedly saying “fantastic” and validating a false premise with “You’re absolutely right to be confused.”
