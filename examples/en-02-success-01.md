# Pattern 2 (en): Undue Emphasis on Notability/Media — Success Case

## Input Text

> The startup has garnered significant attention from investors and industry leaders alike. Its innovative platform has been widely recognized as a game-changer in the fintech space, attracting widespread interest from media outlets around the world. The company has made headlines for its rapid growth and captured the imagination of the tech community.

## Expected Output

> Bloomberg reported in January 2024 that the startup closed a $40 million Series B round led by Andreessen Horowitz. Its payment processing tool handles about 200,000 transactions a day, mostly for small merchants in Southeast Asia.

## Applied Pattern

- Pattern 2 (Undue Emphasis on Notability/Media): "garnered significant attention", "widely recognized", "attracting widespread interest", "made headlines", "captured the imagination of" — five watch-word phrases claiming broad attention without naming a single publication, reporter, or concrete metric.

## Judgment

**Success** — The fire condition ("a claim of broad attention, coverage, or acclaim appears without a named publication, outlet, or specific figure") is met multiple times. Not one of the five notability claims is backed by a named source. The corrected version replaces vague acclaim with a named outlet (Bloomberg), a date, a specific funding figure, a named investor, and usage metrics.
