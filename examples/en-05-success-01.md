# Pattern 5 (en): Vague Attributions — Success Case

## Input Text

> Experts say that electric vehicles will dominate the market within a decade. Studies show that battery costs are declining rapidly, and many believe this trend will accelerate. According to industry sources, several major automakers are planning to phase out combustion engines entirely. Analysts predict that the charging infrastructure gap will be closed by 2030.

## Expected Output

> BloombergNEF's 2024 Electric Vehicle Outlook projects EVs will make up 44% of global car sales by 2030. Lithium-ion battery pack prices fell from $139/kWh in 2023 to an estimated $113/kWh in 2024. Ford and GM have both announced plans to stop selling new gas-only passenger cars by 2035.

## Applied Pattern

- Pattern 5 (Vague Attributions): Four consecutive sentences invoke unnamed authorities — "experts say", "studies show", "many believe", "industry sources", "analysts predict" — without naming a single person, institution, or dated publication.

## Judgment

**Success** — The fire condition ("any claim of authority appears with an unspecified source rather than a named one") is met four times in four sentences. No expert, study, source, or analyst is identified. The corrected version replaces every vague attribution with a named source (BloombergNEF, Ford, GM), a specific publication or announcement, and concrete figures.
