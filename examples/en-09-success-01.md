# Pattern 9 (en): Negative Parallelisms — Success Case

## Input Text

> This program is not just an educational initiative but a transformative force for social change. It goes beyond mere job training to become a holistic community development engine. The impact is not merely economic but profoundly cultural, transcending traditional boundaries to become a model for inclusive growth nationwide.

## Expected Output

> The program trains about 300 people a year in welding, electrical work, and HVAC repair. Graduates earn a median starting wage of $22/hour. The program also runs a weekly community dinner that draws around 150 residents.

## Applied Pattern

- Pattern 9 (Negative Parallelisms): Three "not X but Y" structures in one paragraph: "not just...but a transformative force", "goes beyond...to become", "not merely...but profoundly cultural, transcending...to become." Each could be replaced by a direct positive statement with no loss of meaning.

## Judgment

**Success** — The fire condition ("2+ 'not X but Y' structures in the same document") is met with three instances in a single paragraph. None of the negative framing does real contrastive work — in each case, the positive statement alone would be simpler and equally clear. "It trains 300 people a year" says more than "it is not just job training." The corrected version drops all negative framing and states what the program does, for how many people, and at what wage.
