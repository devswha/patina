# Pattern 10 (en): Rule of Three Overuse — Success Case

## Input Text

> The initiative promotes sustainability, equity, and innovation across all departments. Employees are encouraged to embrace curiosity, resilience, and collaboration in their daily work. Together, these values create a more engaged, productive, and forward-looking organization.

## Expected Output

> The initiative's main goal is cutting the company's carbon footprint 30% by 2027. To get there, each department sets its own quarterly reduction target. The facilities team, for instance, switched to LED lighting last year and cut electricity use by 18%.

## Applied Pattern

- Pattern 10 (Rule of Three Overuse): Three consecutive sentences each contain an arbitrary triple: "sustainability, equity, and innovation", "curiosity, resilience, and collaboration", "engaged, productive, and forward-looking." The count is the same every time, and any of these trios could just as easily be two items or four.

## Judgment

**Success** — The fire condition ("3-item lists appear 2+ times in the same document, or a triple-part sentence where the count is arbitrary") is met three times in three sentences. None of the groupings has a natural reason to be exactly three — "sustainability and equity" would be equally valid, as would adding "transparency" as a fourth. The rhythmic repetition of triples creates a rehearsed feel. The corrected version uses the naturally correct count: one primary goal, one method, and one concrete example with a specific result.
