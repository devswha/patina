# Pattern 12 (en): False Ranges — Success Case

## Input Text

> The conference attracts a diverse audience, from fresh graduates to seasoned executives, spanning everything from cutting-edge AI research to timeless leadership principles. Whether you are a solo entrepreneur or a Fortune 500 CEO, there is something for everyone, ranging from hands-on technical workshops to high-level strategic keynotes.

## Expected Output

> The conference runs two parallel tracks. The technical track covers LLM fine-tuning, retrieval-augmented generation, and on-device inference. The business track focuses on AI procurement, vendor evaluation, and regulatory compliance. Last year, about 60% of attendees were mid-career engineers and the rest were product managers or executives.

## Applied Pattern

- Pattern 12 (False Ranges): Three false ranges in two sentences: "from fresh graduates to seasoned executives", "from cutting-edge AI research to timeless leadership principles", "from hands-on technical workshops to high-level strategic keynotes." Each presents two poles that do not meaningfully bound a spectrum — they are decorative breadth claims, not informative descriptions.

## Judgment

**Success** — The fire condition ("a 'from X to Y' construction appears where the two poles do not meaningfully bound a spectrum — the range is decorative rather than informative") is met three times. "Fresh graduates to seasoned executives" does not tell you who actually attends. "Cutting-edge AI to timeless leadership" defines no useful range — what falls between them? The corrected version replaces decorative ranges with the actual conference structure (two named tracks), specific topics, and concrete attendee demographics.
