# Pattern 25: Metronomic Paragraph Structure — Success (product update)

## Input Text

> Bravelark Analytics has seen strong adoption of its dashboard product this quarter. Weekly active teams increased by 41.3 percent compared to the previous period. This growth demonstrates the platform's expanding role in data-driven decision making.
>
> The company's alerting module has also gained significant traction. Customers configured over 12,000 new alert rules in the last month alone. This uptake highlights the importance of proactive monitoring in modern operations.
>
> Bravelark's export integrations have likewise shown notable momentum. Connections to third-party warehouses doubled over the quarter. This trend underscores the value of interoperability in the analytics ecosystem.

## Expected Output

> The number Bravelark Analytics keeps quoting this quarter is 41.3 percent — that's the jump in weekly active teams using the dashboard. For a product in its third year, that curve is unusual.
>
> Alerting tells a different story. Twelve thousand new alert rules in a month sounds impressive until you learn most of them came from two enterprise customers migrating off a legacy tool.
>
> Exports doubled. Nobody at the company predicted that one; the integrations team had been considered a maintenance backwater until this quarter.

## Applied Pattern

- Pattern 25 (Metronomic Paragraph Structure): All three paragraphs run the same template — product area, growth statistic, then an interpretive significance sentence ("demonstrates," "highlights," "underscores"). Three consecutive paragraphs, identical internal shape.

## Judgment

**Success** — The rewrite breaks the template three different ways: paragraph one leads with the statistic, paragraph two undercuts its own number with context, paragraph three drops the significance sentence entirely and ends on an internal anecdote.
